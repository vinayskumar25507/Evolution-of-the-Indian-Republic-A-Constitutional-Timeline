1858

British Crown Rule

The British Crown took direct control over India, beginning a series of reforms.

1861

Indian Councils Act

Involved Indians in governance by establishing local legislative councils at the provincial level.

1885

Formation of the INC

The Indian National Congress (INC) was formed as the main organization demanding full independence.

1909

Morley-Minto Reforms

Introduced limited elections for Indians to legislative councils and separate electorates.

1919

Rowlatt Act & Gov Act

Introduced Dyarchy. The Rowlatt Act fueled widespread protests like the Jallianwala Bagh Massacre.

1935

Government of India Act

Established a federal system, serving as the primary blueprint for the Indian Constitution's structure.

1942

Quit India Movement

Indian leaders demanded an immediate end to British rule during World War II.

1946

Constituent Assembly

The Cabinet Mission Plan led to the creation of the Constituent Assembly to draft the Constitution.

1947

Indian Independence

India gained independence from British rule on August 15th and was partitioned.

1949

Constitution Adopted

The Constitution of India was formally adopted by the Constituent Assembly on Nov 26th.

1950

Constitution Takes Effect

The Constitution came into effect on January 26th, commemorating Purna Swaraj.
